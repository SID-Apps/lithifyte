People, accounts & statements

Run the whole household from one place: add each person, their accounts (current, credit card or savings — the kind changes how the numbers are counted), and file every statement under its owner. Upload bank exports as CSV (from Excel: File → Save As → CSV) — duplicate rows are detected and skipped, so overlapping exports are safe. Set an account's opening balance and the dashboard reconciles it against reality, flags missing months, and can anchor a linked goal's pot. Everything stays in this browser; nothing is sent anywhere.

AI categoriser — your local model

Point this at any OpenAI-compatible server running on your own machine (Ollama, LM Studio, llama.cpp) and it will suggest categories for whatever the auto-categoriser couldn't handle — biggest money first. Nothing is applied automatically: every suggestion you accept becomes an ordinary rule (first in line, yours to reorder or delete), and nothing ever leaves your machine. Savings, rent and anything gambling-shaped are flagged for your judgement rather than trusted. Ollama users: start with OLLAMA_ORIGINS=* so the browser may call it. Fix the categories

The bank's auto-categorisation gets things wrong. Add a rule here and every chart, KPI and projection on this page recomputes instantly. Rules apply top-to-bottom — the first match wins, and ▲▼ reorders them — and a rule can be limited to an exact amount or a € range (so “AMAZON over €100” can differ from small Amazon spends). The special category “Deposit savings” means “this money counts toward my goals”; anything else is an ordinary expense. If a big monthly transfer is actually rent, change its rule's category to Rent — your true savings rate shows immediately.

Forecast & recurring costs

Similar amounts in 3+ of the last 4 months are treated as monthly; quarterly, 6-monthly and yearly rhythms (insurance renewals, motor tax) are detected across your full history and spread into the forecast. Fine-tune it: set when each one ends (car finance ends Jan 2028…), add items your statements can't see yet, and adjust the safety buffer — the cautious path holds back a share of your historical spending swings, and every goal verdict above uses it. Decembers get your historical uplift automatically.

Beyond the deposit — the wealth engine

The machine you've built shouldn't idle once the deposit is done. Free money you can claim, what your pot earns while it waits, the pension lever (the best guaranteed return available to a 40% taxpayer), and where the surplus flows after every goal is funded. Financial pulse

One dial over the whole household — seven measures across the Spend / Save / Borrow / Plan pillars, each scored against absolute benchmarks (never your own average, so a bad normal can't score as fine) and traceable to the exact numbers behind it. Flow measures follow the window you pick; balance measures (liquidity, long-term, debt, budgets) are as of today. Measures without data are left out and say why — the dial never pretends.

Net worth

Every layer counted once: reconciled cash from your accounts, goal pots that live in accounts this file doesn't track, priced investments, and anything you add below — property, vehicles, a pension valuation — minus tracked debts and credit-card balances. Each manual value carries its as-of date and goes amber as it ages instead of silently pretending to be current; possible double counts are flagged, never guessed away.

Loans & debt

Track any mortgage, loan or card balance — even ones whose payments never appear in your statements. Enter the payment or an end date; exact amortisation derives the rest: payoff date, interest remaining, and the month the freed payment flows back into your forecast surplus. Commitments feed the mortgage-readiness capacity check. If the repayments are already in your statements, pick the merchant so the forecast ends them on time instead of double-counting.

Investments & crypto

Your positions — shares, ETFs, funds, crypto, pension — priced from a quotes book you control. Quotes never leave this file: type them in (always works), hit ↻ for crypto (fetched keylessly from CoinGecko when this page is opened as a local file; the hosted artifact blocks external requests, so there it stays manual), or 📥 import a quotes-….json written by the feeder CLI (node feeder.mjs prices AAPL VWCE=VWCE.DE — keyless Yahoo quotes converted to EUR at ECB rates). Each row shows the estimated Irish tax if you sold today — CGT for shares & crypto, exit tax for funds — and fund lots carry their 8-year deemed-disposal date, the one Revenue bill people forget.

Data integrity
    Self-test running…

      Backup & restore

      Everything you configure — rules, goals, people, uploaded statements, recurring fine-tunes, colours — lives only in this browser. Export it to a file you keep; import it on any device (or after clearing browser data) to restore everything. Downloaded page + your backup file = fully self-hosted.

      Passphrase

      With a passphrase set, exports are AES-encrypted (same passphrase needed to import) and “Lock” keeps every store encrypted at rest in this browser — you'll be asked for the passphrase whenever the page opens. There is no recovery from a forgotten passphrase; keep an unencrypted backup somewhere safe if unsure.

      Danger zone

      Erases everything this page stores in this browser — uploaded statements, rules, goals, people & accounts, budgets, notes, colours and settings — and returns it to a blank slate. Export a backup first; there is no undo.

      How to update with new transactions (for you, or any AI assistant)

      This page is self-contained: transactions, rules, goals, people, accounts, recurring fine-tuning and simulator levers live in a single JSON block (<script id="finance-data">), and every number is derived from it in the browser. Statements you upload live in this browser's storage — attach your backup file (Export everything, above) or the original CSVs when asking an AI to update the file. Say:

      Here is my dashboard HTML and my transactions CSVs (same columns as before). Follow the "FOR AI ASSISTANTS" comment at the top of the HTML: map the CSV rows into the "transactions" array (tagging each row with its accountId), keep/merge my "rules", "goals", "users", "accounts", "categories" and "recurring" arrays, and re-assess — goal verdicts, forecast, leaks, and rewrite the static advice cards if the picture has changed.

      To bake in everything you configured here (rules, categories, goals, people, recurring end-dates), use the “Copy re-assess prompt” button in the rules section — it includes all of it automatically.

      Notes on the data: Apr 2024 shows near-zero income because that month's wages landed in May (double payment). “Rent” in the raw data is irregular (two lump payments of €3,000/€2,500 to the same payee) — worth confirming how housing is actually paid, since a lender will ask the same question. Forecasts assume detected recurring items continue until the end date you set. Scheme details (Help to Buy, First Home Scheme, deposit rates) change — verify current terms before relying on them.
