Privacy policy

Last updated: 18 July 2026

The short version: your financial data — statements, transactions, balances, merchants, goals — never leaves your browser. There is no finance backend that could receive it. On the hosted app we store your email for sign-in, and may record privacy-safe product usage events (for example “opened budgets”, not “spent €42 at a shop”). Self-hosted copies send nothing.

Who we are

Lithifyte is a household finance dashboard built by SID Labs. The app runs at app.lithifyte.com, sign-in at access.lithifyte.com, and this marketing site at lithifyte.com. The source code is public at github.com/SID-Apps/lithifyte under the AGPL-3.0 licence, so every claim on this page can be verified by reading the code.

Your financial data: never collected

Lithifyte is a client-only application. All computation — importing statements, categorising spending, budgets, forecasts, net worth — happens in your browser. Your data is kept in your browser's local storage (localStorage and IndexedDB) on your device, and in backup files you export yourself.

  • There is no application backend and no API that accepts financial data.
  • Bank statements you import are parsed locally and never uploaded.
  • Optional encrypted backups use your browser's built-in Web Crypto; the passphrase never leaves your device.
  • The optional AI categoriser talks to a model you run (for example Ollama or LM Studio on your own machine). Pointing it elsewhere is your choice, not a default.
  • You can confirm all of this by watching your browser's network tab while using the app.

What we do collect: identity and product usage (hosted only)

If you use the hosted product and sign in, we store:

  • Your email address, with timestamps of first sign-up, last sign-in link request, last sign-in, and last activity.
  • A session token (cookie lf_session) so you stay signed in for up to 30 days.
  • Privacy-safe product events from the hosted app only — allowlisted names such as sign-in, session start, onboarding step completed, section opened (e.g. map / budget), and actions like “CSV uploaded” or “backup exported”. Events carry short enums only (never amounts, merchants, descriptions, account names, or statement contents).
  • Optional locale preference you choose in Settings (IE or GLOBAL) so we know which region pack to improve.

Sign-in uses magic links — we never ask for or store a password, and we never ask for bank credentials. Email and usage data are used to operate access, understand activation and retention, send product emails you opt into later, and improve the product. They are not sold. Downloaded or self-hosted copies of the app do not phone home.

Cookies

We set one first-party cookie: lf_session, an HttpOnly session cookie scoped to lithifyte.com, used to keep you signed in. There are no advertising cookies and no third-party analytics cookies.

Analytics and tracking

We do not use Google Analytics, advertising pixels, or fingerprinting. On app.lithifyte.com only, the app may send the allowlisted product events above to our Cloudflare Worker at access.lithifyte.com. That is first-party product analytics for retention and product decisions — not a finance data pipeline. See the open-source worker and docs/product-analytics.md in the repository for the exact allowlist.

Infrastructure

Our web pages and the sign-in service are served by Cloudflare. Like any host, Cloudflare processes standard connection logs (such as IP addresses) to deliver the site and defend it against abuse. The marketing pages load fonts from Google Fonts and a small script library from unpkg (a CDN); the finance app itself loads nothing external.

Data retention and deletion

Your email record is kept while you have access. To have it deleted, open an issue at github.com/SID-Apps/lithifyte/issues (or use the contact route in the repository's SECURITY.md) and we will remove it. Your financial data needs no deletion request — it is on your device, and the app's own erase all / delete account controls remove it locally.

Changes

If this policy changes, the new version will be published here with an updated date. Because the sites and the sign-in worker are open source, the history of every change is public in the repository.
